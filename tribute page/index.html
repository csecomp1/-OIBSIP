History

1971 :Attended Reed College in Portland, Oregon, but dropped out after one semester.

1973 : Traveled to India in search of spiritual enlightenment.

1976 : Co-founded Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak.

1977 : The Apple I, Apple's first personal computer, was released.

1980 : Apple went public and Jobs became a multimillionaire.

1983 : The Macintosh, Apple's first graphical user interface (GUI) computer, was released.

1985 : Jobs was forced out of Apple by the company's board of directors.

1986 : Co-founded NeXT, a computer company that focused on educational and business markets.

1989 : Pixar, a computer animation studio, was acquired by Lucasfilm. Jobs became the majority shareholder and CEO.

1996 : Apple acquired NeXT for $400 million. Jobs returned to Apple as interim CEO.

1997 : Jobs was named permanent CEO of Apple.

2001 : The iPod, Apple's first portable digital music player, was released.

2003 : The iTunes Music Store was launched.

2007 : The iPhone, Apple's first smartphone, was released.

2010 : The iPad, Apple's first tablet computer, was released.

2011 : Jobs resigned as CEO of Apple and was succeeded by Tim Cook.

2011 : Died on October 5 from complications related to pancreatic cancer.
